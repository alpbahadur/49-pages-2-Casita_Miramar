Three Generations of Flavor

Welcome to Casita Miramar, where the culinary legacy of the Pérez family comes alive. Founded in 2010 by Leonardo Pérez and his late father Jesús Manuel Pérez, our restaurant is housed in a charming 1920s villa that was once the Hotel Toro.

We are the sister restaurant to the iconic La Casita Blanca, which recently received the prestigious James Beard Foundation's "America's Classics Award." Our recipes, rooted in grandmother Aurora Ruiz's traditional cooking, bring you authentic Puerto Rican comida criolla in an atmosphere of warmth and nostalgia.

From the moment you arrive with our complimentary plantain soup and bacalaítos, to your farewell shot of chichaíto, every detail is crafted to make you feel like family.

From Our Farm to Your Table

At Casita Miramar, we believe the best meals start with the best ingredients. That's why we source fresh, local products from our own family farm in Manatí.

Every plantain, every herb, every vegetable carries the story of Puerto Rican soil and the dedication of three generations. This connection to the land infuses our dishes with authentic flavors that transport you to the heart of the island.

Fresh. Local. Authentic. Always.
